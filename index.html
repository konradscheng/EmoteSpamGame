What Is Our Project?

EmoteWizard is a game for streamers that allows twitch chat to help them make in-game decisions. It involves a hero, who travels the world happily, but eventually encounters Imaqtpie and is forced to engage in a fierce battle. Initially, the streamer tries to handle this beast on his own, only to realize that Imaqtpie's raw power is overwelhming him/her. With no other options, the streamer turns to his/her chat for help, in the form of emote spams.

Why We Built This

As avid Twitch viewers, we all agree that steamers, especially larger ones, have difficulty interacting with chat and allowing everyone to participate in activities. Playing this game would allow non-subs to be involved in viewer games as well as giving streamers another way to learn about their viewers. Furthermore, before this project, our team did not have much experience with Python so we wanted to work on a project that would allow us to learn the language as well as how to work with the Twitch APIs. Ultimately, we believe that this game, when expanded upon and polished, can become a product that all steamers and viewers can enjoy.

How It Works

When the streamer engages an enemy, the game prompts twitch chat to spam certain emotes to help the streamer make a decision. Our program would take in each line of text as a string and determine how many of each emote were spammed. After a certain amount of time, the game makes a decision based on the emote that was spammed the most.
